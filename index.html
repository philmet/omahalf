What is this

Basically just a social run with a fancy name

A few friends looked at the 2026 calendar and couldn't find a run in June. So, we're organising our own. Turns out it is a half marathon from Omaha Beach to Matakana Village via Point Wells. Then we thought: other people might want to do this too. So here we are. No timing chips, no pre-race bib pick-up, no finisher's banana (well, maybe there will be bananas).

The route is genuinely beautiful though — Omaha to Point Wells, then through the Matakana Cycle Trail to the village. If you're going to run 21 km in 2026, this is a pretty good way to do it.

The general plan

Start north end Omaha Beach (shoes on)
Run to Omaha Beach south end
Loop back & west/north to Point Wells
Then run the Matakana Cycle Trail
Finish in Matakana Village
Eat something. Drink something.
You've earned it.

Reasonable questions

Yes, it's really free

Still unsure? Email us at [email protected] and we'll try to help.

When is it?
Sunday 7 June 2025. Morning start — exact time TBC, but we'll let everyone know once they've signed up. Fingers crossed for good weather.
Where does it start and finish?
Starts at Omaha Beach, finishes in Matakana Village. They're about 9 km apart by road so you'll need to sort your own way back to your car — carpooling recommended, or park in Matakana and get a ride to the start.
Is there actually an entry fee?
Nope. This isn't a real race. There's no timing, no medals, no chip, no official anything. Just let us know you're coming so we have a rough idea of numbers.
What shoes should I wear?
Road shoes are fine. About a third of the course is the Matakana Cycle Trail (compacted dirt) but nothing gnarly. If you have trail shoes, great. If not, don't stress.
Do I have to run the whole thing?
Absolutely not. Walk, run, jog, shuffle. There's no cut-off, no one's timing you, and nobody is watching. The only rule is that you get yourself to Matakana eventually.
Can I bring my dog?
Honestly, yes — but check the trail rules for dogs off-lead. Probably keep them on a lead for the road sections. A dog running a half marathon is very on-brand for this event.
